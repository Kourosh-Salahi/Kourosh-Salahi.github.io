Overview & Key Idea

The apparent “distortion” in portraits and the “compression” in telephoto scenes arise primarily from camera position (perspective), not the lens itself. Changing focal length changes framing; changing distance changes perspective. Below, I reproduce three setups that illustrate this distinction.

Reflection & Takeaways

  • Perspective is governed by camera position. Focal length changes framing and sampling, not relative geometry.
  • Portraits generally benefit from more working distance — more flattering proportions.
  • Telephoto “compression” is the distant viewpoint visualized.
  • The dolly zoom keeps subject size constant while perspective shifts underneath — a dramatic contrast cue.

Appendix — Workflow Notes

  3. Edits: Minor global exposure/white balance; no geometry warps.
